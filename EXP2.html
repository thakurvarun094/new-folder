Rohit Sharma Creates World Record; Becomes First Player In History Since Viv Richards 36 Years Ago To...


Rohit Sharma set a massive world record during his knock of 73 in 97 balls against Australia in the second ODI during India's two-wicket defeat. Rohit completed a huge milestone while going past Viv Richards in a rare list of players.

Rohit Sharma etched his name in the history books by creating a new world record as he proved his critics wrong during the second ODI of the three-match series between India and Australia at the Adelaide Oval on Thursday (October 23) with a fighting knock of 73 in 97 balls.

With Yashasvi Jaiswal breathing down his neck and a failure in the first ODI, the pressure on the former skipper was removed from captaincy before the tour. Despite making a scrappy start, Rohit rode the storm and ensured India were able to avoid a collapse after Shubman Gill and Virat Kohli's early departure. Rohit Sharma Sets Massive World Record Through the course of his innings, Rohit set a huge world record as he completed 1000 runs against Australia on Australian soil. While Rohit has 1409 runs on Australian soil overall in ODI Cricket, a total of 1071 of them have come against the home team with a stunning average of 56.36 and a strike-rate of 89.32 with four centuries and three half-centuries.

Overall, Rohit is only the fifth player from a visiting team to score over 1000 runs against the Australian team in Australia. Meanwhile, the 39-year-old is the only eighth player to have crossed the landmark against an opponent away from home.

In this list of eight players, Rohit has both the best average and strike-rate, breaking a record Viv Richards held for a long time. Infact, Rohit is the only player apart from Viv Richards to average over 50 while scoring over 1000 runs against a team in the opponent's home country.
